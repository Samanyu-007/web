Publications

FPGA-based decision-making for satellite orientation

Abstract: Field Programmable Gate Arrays (FPGAs) are board-based hardware implementations of Very Large-Scale Integration (VLSI) that have greatly flexible computing capabilities. To show its ability to perform multifarious functions together, this paper proposes the design for a prototype that uses light input from multiple onboard light sensors around the device or satellite. This information is used in terms of the intensity of light detected by the sensors and their associated angles of reading to activate the actuators involved in the orientation of the solar panels and cold gas thrusters to get the most efficient positioning for sunlight to be the best incident on the solar panels. This is an alternative name to the use of FPGA in space technology other than for the currently prevalent data processing. The goal of this paper is to promote the multifunctional use of FPGAs in the vast field of space technology and its scope for the furtherment of human exploration of outer space.

A robotic solution for internal imperfection detection

Abstract: The primary edge that robots give over manual labor in industries, in addition to human safety due to automation, is their mobility and size. Robots can be programmed for the perfection of their tasks. In this paper, we will devise a multi-sensing machine that can be built up to the requirements of the client's machines. This research proposes a low-cost solution for avoidable issues. The solution proposed in this paper detects the anomalies in a machine's internal workings with the help of a concise robot that makes it useful and efficient for the secure and safe functioning of the systems.
